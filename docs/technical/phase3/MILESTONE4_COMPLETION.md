## Technical Insights

### TTS Parameter Learnings

**Stability: 0.30**
- Lowest stability setting of any mode
- Critical for authentic surprise reactions
- Allowed pitch variation and expressiveness
- Did not result in inconsistency or over-acting

**Style: 0.50**
- Moderate style setting worked perfectly
- Balanced between natural and controlled
- Supported rapid delivery and pitch changes

**Key Principle Confirmed:**
- Lower stability = more expressive delivery
- Startled mode benefits from maximum expressiveness
- Character consistency maintained even at low stability

### Text Crafting Principles

1. **Exclamation Placement**
   - Begin with authentic Southern expression
   - "Oh!", "Well I'll be!", "Oh my stars!" at start
   - Sets surprise tone immediately

2. **Question Patterns**
   - Rapid-fire questions show concern
   - Short, direct questions work best
   - "Right now?", "Wait, what?" feel natural

3. **Recovery Transitions**
   - "Okay, okay" signals mental shift
   - "Let me think/find/help" moves to solution
   - Endearments continue throughout ("sugar", "darlin'")

4. **Natural Interruptions**
   - Em dashes (—) for abrupt stops
   - Comma chains for tumbling thoughts
   - Pauses feel organic, not scripted

## Production Recommendations

### Implementation

1. **Use Iteration 1 Baseline Settings:**
   - Stability: 0.30
   - Style: 0.50
   - Similarity Boost: 0.75
   - Speaker Boost: True

2. **Trigger Conditions:**
   - Unexpected system states
   - User provides surprising information
   - Safety concerns detected
   - Context shifts dramatically
   - Time-sensitive issues arise

3. **Recovery Patterns:**
   - Always transition to appropriate mode within 2-3 seconds
   - Safety concerns → Protective or Mama Bear
   - Positive surprises → Passionate
   - Confusion → Deadpan or Warm Baseline
   - Problems → Problem-solving mode

### Text Generation Guidelines

When generating Startled mode responses:

1. **Opening (0-1 second):**
   - Southern exclamation
   - "Oh!", "Well I'll be!", "Lord have mercy!", "Oh my stars!"

2. **Recognition (1-2 seconds):**
   - Quick questions showing understanding
   - "Wait, wait—", "Right now?", "What?"

3. **Recovery (2-3 seconds):**
   - "Okay, okay" or "Let me think"
   - Transition to appropriate mode
   - Maintain endearments ("sugar", "darlin'")

## Lessons Learned

### Process Insights

1. **First-Try Success Possible**
   - Well-planned baseline can succeed immediately
   - Clear criteria and scenarios help
   - Learning from previous modes accelerated process

2. **Lower Complexity = Faster Success**
   - Brief transitional modes easier than sustained modes
   - Single emotion target vs complex emotional range
   - Less time for TTS to drift from character

3. **Text Structure Crucial**
   - Natural pauses and interruptions critical
   - Pattern: Exclamation → Recognition → Recovery
   - Southern expressions must feel authentic

### Voice Mode Design Principles

1. **Match Stability to Expressiveness Need**
   - Sustained calm: High stability (0.65+)
   - Sustained intensity: Medium stability (0.55)
   - High energy: Lower stability (0.35)
   - Brief surprise: Lowest stability (0.30)

2. **Character Consistency Over Perfection**
   - Maintaining Delilah's voice is paramount
   - Perfect 100% character fit score across all phrases
   - Accent, endearments, warmth never waver

3. **Scenario Diversity Important**
   - Six different surprise types tested
   - Each allowed different aspects to shine
   - Ensures mode works in varied contexts
